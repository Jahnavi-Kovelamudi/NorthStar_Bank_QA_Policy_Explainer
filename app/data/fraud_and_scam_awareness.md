# NorthStar Bank Fraud and Scam Awareness Guidance

NorthStar Bank encourages customers to stay alert to fraud, phishing, impersonation scams, and unauthorized account activity. Fraud attempts may occur by phone, email, text message, social media, or fake websites.

## Common Warning Signs
Fraudsters often create urgency, ask customers to act immediately, or pretend to be bank employees, government representatives, or merchants. Suspicious requests for passwords, debit card PINs, one-time passcodes, or full account numbers should be treated as high risk.

## Safe Customer Practices
Customers should never share online banking passwords, debit card PINs, one-time verification codes, or security answers through insecure channels. Customers should verify suspicious calls or messages by contacting NorthStar Bank through official phone numbers or website channels.

## Suspicious Transactions
Customers who notice unfamiliar transfers, card usage, or login alerts should promptly review their account activity and contact the bank. If the customer believes an account has been compromised, they should use official banking channels to secure access and report the issue.

## Assistant Guidance
This assistant may provide general fraud-awareness education and safety recommendations. It cannot investigate an active fraud case, verify a transaction, or collect confidential credentials from customers.
